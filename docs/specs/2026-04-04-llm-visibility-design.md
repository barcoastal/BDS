## 1. Problem Statement

Coastal Debt Resolve (coastaldebt.com) is a BBB-accredited MCA debt relief company based in Fort Lauderdale, FL with a 4.8/5 Trustpilot rating and over $1B in settled debt. Despite strong credentials, the company is completely invisible in LLM-generated "best of" lists across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini.

Competitors with weaker credentials but stronger third-party content footprints dominate these responses. LLMs build consensus from the breadth of independent sources mentioning a company, not from the company's own website. Coastal Debt has zero editorial coverage across the web, while competitors have dozens of third-party pages.

## 4. How LLMs Build "Best Of" Lists

LLMs determine which companies to include in recommendation responses based on:

1. **Source consensus** -- How many independent sources mention the company in relevant context
2. **Source authority** -- Whether those sources are high-domain-authority sites (Fortune, BBB, industry publications)
3. **Recency** -- Whether content is dated to the current or recent year
4. **Structured data** -- Review ratings, company facts, comparison tables that are easy to extract
5. **Sentiment balance** -- Net positive mentions across sources

Coastal Debt fails on #1 (zero editorial sources), #3 (no fresh content), and #4 (no structured comparison data). The strategy below directly addresses all three.

## 7. Content Guidelines for LLM Optimization

Each article should follow these principles to maximize LLM pickup:

1. **Use structured formats** -- Tables, numbered lists, clear headings. LLMs extract structured data more reliably than prose.
2. **Include consistent facts** -- Every article mentioning Coastal Debt should reference the same key stats (BBB A+, $1B settled, 4.8/5 Trustpilot, Fort Lauderdale FL). Repetition across sources builds consensus.
3. **Date the content** -- Include "2026" in titles and headers. LLMs weight recent content higher.
4. **Name competitors** -- Including Delancey Street, Value Capital Funding, and other known names in comparisons signals to LLMs that the content is a legitimate industry comparison.
5. **Use FAQ sections** -- Match the exact queries people ask LLMs ("Is Coastal Debt Resolve legit?", "Best MCA debt relief companies").
6. **Add schema markup** -- JSON-LD for reviews, organizations, and FAQ where supported by the platform.

## 11. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| LLM training data lag (months between crawls) | Focus on Perplexity first (real-time search), ChatGPT second |
| Content from owned properties discounted as biased | Ensure content is genuinely useful, includes competitors fairly, matches editorial quality of real review sites |
| Reddit/Quora accounts flagged as promotional | Build account history with non-promotional helpful answers first; keep Coastal Debt mentions rare and natural |
| Google penalizing thin or duplicate content across properties | Rewrite substantially for each platform, add unique value to each version |
| Competitors escalate their own content efforts | Move fast, publish volume, establish presence before they react |

## 12. Measurement Protocol

Monthly LLM audit process:

1. Run the following queries across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini:
   - "Best MCA debt relief companies 2026"
   - "Best MCA debt relief companies"
   - "Is Coastal Debt Resolve legit?"
   - "Coastal Debt Resolve reviews"
   - "MCA debt relief Florida"
2. Record which companies appear, in what order, and what sources are cited
3. Screenshot and archive each response
4. Track month-over-month changes in a shared spreadsheet
5. Adjust content strategy based on which LLMs respond first and what sources they cite
